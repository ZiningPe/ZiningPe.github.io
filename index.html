About Me

I am a first-year PhD student at the Division of Public Policy, HKUST, investigating the intersection of Deep Learning, Large Language Models, and 3D Visualization to tackle complex urban planning challenges and facilitate Evidence-based Planning.

My research sits at the crossroads of spatial econometrics, environmental economics, and urban science. I am particularly interested in how novel data sources and machine learning methods can unlock new insights into city dynamics, green investment, and sustainable development.

Previously, I completed my Master's degree at Kyushu University (Japan) on a Monbukagakusho (JASSO) Honors Scholarship, where I developed rigorous quantitative methods for environmental and regional economics.
